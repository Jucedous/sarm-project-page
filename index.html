Semantic Object Transport

Context-Aware Safety Evaluation and Control for Semantic Object Transport

SARM treats semantic safety as a graded planning signal. It keeps hard geometric feasibility separate from soft context-dependent exposure, so a robot can detour around risks such as liquid near electronics or sharp objects near fragile targets without turning every hazard into an impossible hard block.

Overview

Semantic risk changes how transport should be executed.

A path that is collision-free can still be unsafe when the carried object changes the meaning of nearby interactions. Carrying a drink near electronics, a knife near balloons or people, or a heavy object near fragile items should affect search, smoothing, replanning, and validation.

SARM represents those hazards as pairwise semantic rules with a relation type, severity weight, and preferred soft clearance. The rules are grounded into continuous workspace risk fields and reused by the planner, local control, visualization, and post-hoc metrics.

Positioning

Responsible manipulation benchmarks and policy-level safety methods focus on recognizing risky tasks and selecting responsible actions. SARM focuses on the motion layer: the geometry of the trajectory is shaped by semantic exposure while hard collision and boundary constraints remain strictly enforced.

Method

One risk representation, several planning roles.

01

Pairwise Hazard Rules

Object categories and tags activate relationships such as liquid-electronic, sharp-fragile, heavy-fragile, and robot-human.

02

Continuous Risk Field

Each active relation becomes a distance-dependent exposure field, allowing semantic safety to be optimized as a soft cost.

03

Geometric Shield

Physical obstacles, workspace boundaries, robot body clearance, and carried-object footprint remain hard admissibility constraints.

04

Risk-Aware Control

Search, smoothing, MPC-style detours, dynamic replanning, and dense validation use the same semantic exposure signal. The heatmap camera view remains visible throughout each rollout; only the colored heat zone and exposure trace activate after the carried object is grasped.
