Semantic-Aware Safety Evaluation and Planning

SARM converts contextual object relationships into continuous semantic risk fields. Motion backends retain their own hard feasibility checks while using the same risk signal for search, post-processing, replanning, and dense trajectory validation.

Overview

Semantic safety is graded, contextual, and backend-modular.

A geometrically collision-free trajectory can still be unsafe when the transported object changes the consequences of nearby interactions—for example, liquid near electronics, a sharp object near a person or fragile item, or a heavy object near fragile items.

SARM represents these contextual hazards as pairwise rules containing a relation type, severity weight, and preferred soft clearance. Each active rule is grounded into a smooth workspace risk contribution, producing a bounded query r(q,t) ∈ [0,1] that can be reused by different motion-generation backends.

Core separation

Soft semantic exposure, hard backend feasibility.

Semantic risk shapes the trajectory as a graded cost. Collision, workspace, robot-body, and execution constraints remain the responsibility of the selected backend and are used as hard screening checks during planning. Dense validation separately reports sampled margin violations.

Method

One semantic risk interface, multiple motion backends.

01

Pairwise hazard construction

Activate contextual relations between the carried object, robot body, and scene objects, such as liquid–electronic, sharp–human, heavy–fragile, and robot–human.

02

Continuous risk grounding

Convert soft-clearance residuals into smooth distance-dependent risk values, weighted by the severity of each semantic relation.

03

Backend-specific feasibility

Use a planar CBF-style clearance backend for navigation and sampled Cartesian workspace/signed-distance checks for carried-object manipulation.

04

Planning and validation

Query the same semantic signal during A* search, shortcut smoothing, receding-horizon planning, dynamic replanning, and dense final-trajectory evaluation.

Pairwise rule

hij = (oi, oj, αij, wij, dijsoft)

Soft-clearance residual

εij(q,t) = dij(q,t) − dijsoft

Smooth pairwise risk

r̂ij(q,t) = σ(−εij(q,t) / ηij)

Qualitative videos

Whole-arm simulation examples across semantic contexts.

Scope of these videos. They are supplemental whole-arm simulations that reuse the SARM risk field with backend-specific IK, collision, and guarded waypoint execution. They are not the seeded 60-trial offline Cartesian benchmark summarized above. The plots embedded in the videos are online demo diagnostics and should not be read as the aggregate paper-table metrics.

Object-dependent hazard activation

Soda can and dry sponge over a laptop

The matched-view comparison shows that the same laptop scene can induce different risk fields depending on the transported object: the dry sponge remains semantically neutral, while the liquid-bearing can activates the liquid–electronic relation.

Gaming desktop: direct transfer vs SARM-MPC

Sharp object near fragile objects

Knife and balloons: guarded semantic detour

The direct pass enters the strongest shared risk region. The SARM backend uses a wider waypoint route around the balloon pair while preserving the same start and target.

Flame near balloon, paper, and book

Candle transport: complete pickup-to-place operation

The candle is grasped, lifted, carried, and placed upright. A guarded SARM detour reduces exposure to the central semantic field while the whole-arm backend maintains execution feasibility.
